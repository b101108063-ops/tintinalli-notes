# Approaches

This chapter covers approaches and chief complaints in rheumatology.

---

## Diagnostic Approach to Joint Pain

### Step 1: Consider Nonarticular Mimics of Joint Pain

| Structure Type | Examples |
|--------------|---------|
| **Articular structures** | Synovium, synovial fluid, articular cartilage, intraarticular ligaments, joint capsule, juxtaarticular bone |
| **Nonarticular or periarticular structures** | Extraarticular ligaments, tendons, bursae, muscle, fascia, bone, nerve, overlying skin |

---

### Step 2: Characterize the Joint Pain

**Temporality:**
- **Acute:** <6 weeks
- **Chronic:** >6 weeks

**Inflammation:** Determine if inflammatory or noninflammatory by assessing for inflammatory features:

| Assessment | Findings Suggesting Inflammation |
|------------|-------------------------------|
| **History** | Morning stiffness lasting >30–60 minutes |
| **Exam** | "Rubor, calor, tumor" — periarticular erythema, warmth, joint swelling |
| **Labs** | Elevated ESR and/or CRP, synovial fluid WBC >2K cells/μL |

**Number of joints involved:**
- **Monoarthritis:** Single joint
- **Oligoarthritis:** 2–4 joints
- **Polyarthritis:** ≥5 joints

---

### Step 3: Analyze Synovial Fluid

Synovial fluid analysis is particularly critical in patients with acute monoarthritis.

| WBC Count | Interpretation |
|-----------|----------------|
| **<2K cells/μL** | Noninflammatory. If bloody: trauma, coagulopathy, tumor. If non-bloody: OA, avascular necrosis, Charcot arthropathy. |
| **2–10K cells/μL** | Inflammatory arthritis, gout, and pseudogout. |
| **>20K cells/μL** | Very high concern for infectious arthritis; gout and pseudogout also possible. Orthopedic surgical consultation for washout is essential. |

**Crystal analysis:**
- **Monosodium urate (gout):** Needle-shaped, negatively birefringent crystals (yellow when parallel to polarization axis).
- **Calcium pyrophosphate dihydrate (pseudogout):** Rhomboid crystals with weak positive birefringence (blue when parallel to polarization axis).

---

### Differential Diagnosis for Joint Pain Based on Clinical Characteristics

| Category | Inflammatory | Noninflammatory |
|----------|-------------|-----------------|
| **Acute Monoarthritis** | Gout, pseudogout, septic arthritis, gonococcal arthritis | Bony/ligamentous trauma, hemarthrosis, osteonecrosis/AVN |
| **Acute Oligoarthritis** | Disseminated gonococcal infection, rheumatic fever, acute leukemia | Sickle vaso-occlusive crises |
| **Acute Polyarthritis** | HIV, HBV, HCV, parvovirus B19, chikungunya, rubella, acute leukemia | Type II hyperlipoproteinemia (migratory, episodic) |
| **Chronic Monoarthritis** | Borrelia (Lyme), mycobacterial, fungal | OA (knee/hip), primary bone tumor, bony metastases |
| **Chronic Oligoarthritis** | Axial spondyloarthritis, peripheral spondyloarthritis (reactive arthritis, IBD-associated), sarcoidosis (especially knee), AAV, Whipple's disease | OA (hand/knee/hip), DISH, infiltrative diseases (amyloid, hemochromatosis, Wilson's disease) |
| **Chronic Polyarthritis** | RA, PsA, CTDs (SLE, Sjögren's, SSc, PM/DM, MCTD), CPPD, pseudo-RA | CPPD pseudo-OA, infiltrative diseases (amyloid, hemochromatosis, Wilson's disease) |

| **Venous AND arterial thrombosis** | APLS, Behçet's | Lupus anticoagulant testing, anti-β2GP1, anticardiolipin |

## ANA-Associated Connective Tissue Disorders

Rheumatologic conditions associated with a positive ANA:
1. Systemic lupus erythematosus (SLE)
2. Systemic sclerosis (SSc)
3. Myositis (often ANA-negative; approximately two-thirds of dermatomyositis/polymyositis cases are ANA-positive)
4. Mixed connective tissue disease (MCTD)
5. Primary Sjögren's

---

## ANA Testing Pearls

Antinuclear antibodies (ANA) are autoantibodies that bind to the contents of the cell nucleus. The ANA test detects ANA autoantibodies present in the patient's serum.

**Important considerations:**
- ANA can be elevated in many disorders including autoimmune conditions, infection, and malignancy.
- A small proportion of healthy women have a positive ANA, so it is nonspecific and should only be sent if clinical suspicion is high.
- The level of autoantibody is reported as a titer (highest dilution of serum at which autoantibodies are still detectable; e.g., 1:640 is more dilute than 1:80, suggesting more autoantibodies).
- Probability of autoimmunity increases with higher ANA titers:
  - ANA ≥1:80 is required by EULAR/ACR SLE classification criteria.
  - ANA ≥1:640 is highly specific for SLE.
- If an ELISA-based ANA test is negative but clinical suspicion for CTD is high, ask the lab to perform an immunofluorescence (IF) microscopy-based ANA test, which is more specific.

### Test Characteristics and Clinical Associations for ANA and Sub-Serologies

| Antibody (anti-X) | Target Antigen | Prevalence | Clinical Associations |
|-------------------|---------------|-----------|---------------------|
| **ANA** | Various | ~95% in SLE; ~90% in SSc; >70% in Sjögren's; >50% in PM/DM | Nonspecific; best screening test for SLE |
| **Anti-dsDNA** | dsDNA | ~70% in SLE | ~95% specific for SLE. Titer tracks with disease activity. Associated with nephritis risk. |
| **Anti-Sm** | Proteins binding small noncoding RNAs | ~25% in SLE | ~99% specific for SLE |
| **Anti-U1RNP** | U1 RNP | ~40% in SLE | ~95% in MCTD |
| **Anti-Ro (SSA)** | SSA | ~30–50% in SLE; ~30–40% in Sjögren's | Annular subacute cutaneous lupus rash; congenital heart block. Seen in primary Sjögren's > RA-associated secondary Sjögren's. |
| **Anti-La (SSB)** | SSB | ~15% in SLE; ~25–35% in Sjögren's | Rarely seen without Anti-Ro. Lower risk of nephritis. |
| **Anti-histone** | Chromatin-associated histones | ~95% in drug-induced lupus; ~50–70% in SLE | Drug-induced lupus associated with procainamide, hydralazine, isoniazid |
| **Anti-centromere** | Chromosomal kinetochore proteins | ~70% in SSc | ~95% in CREST (limited cutaneous SSc) |
| **Anti-Scl70** | Topoisomerase I | ~30% in SSc | Diffuse cutaneous SSc + ILD |
| **Anti-RNA Pol III** | RNA Pol III | ~4–10% in SSc | Paraneoplastic SSc (+ test should trigger malignancy workup); positive in most patients with scleroderma renal crisis |
| **Anti-Mi2** | Helicase | ~15–30% in DM | DM with fulminant skin > muscle involvement; excellent treatment response; lower risk of associated malignancy |
| **Anti-PM/Scl** | Nucleolar exosome complex | ~4–12% of SSc | PM/SSc overlap syndrome with ILD, non-erosive arthritis, Raynaud's, mechanic's hands |
