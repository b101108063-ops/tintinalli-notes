# Anatomy and Physiology

This chapter covers anatomy and physiology in rheumatology.

---

## Axial and Appendicular Skeleton

The skeletal system is divided into two main components:

| Component | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Axial skeleton** | Forms the central axis of the body. Includes the bones of the skull, ossicles of the middle ear, hyoid bone of the throat, vertebral column, and thoracic cage (rib cage). |
| **Appendicular skeleton** | Consists of the bones of the pectoral limbs, pelvic limbs, and pelvic girdle. |

---

## Synovial Joint Anatomy

Understanding the anatomy of synovial joints is fundamental to rheumatology:

| Structure | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Joint capsule (articular capsule)** | Envelope surrounding a synovial joint that connects to the periosteum. Each capsule has two layers: an outer fibrous layer and an inner membrane lined by synovium. |
| **Synovial fluid** | Viscous fluid secreted by synovial cells that lubricates the joint. |
| **Articular cartilage** | Thin layer of hyaline cartilage composed of type II collagen, proteoglycans, and water that covers the articulating surface of each bone. |
| **Enthesis** | The site at which tendons or ligaments insert into the bone. |
