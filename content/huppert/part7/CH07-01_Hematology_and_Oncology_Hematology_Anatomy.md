# Hematology Anatomy

## Hematopoiesis

Hematopoietic stem cells can differentiate into either a myeloid or lymphoid lineage.

*(See Figure 7.1 for normal human hematopoiesis.)*

### Myeloid Lineage

The myeloid lineage gives rise to several cell types:

- **Megakaryocytes**: Produce thrombocytes (platelets), which are critical for clotting. Platelets lack nuclei and have a lifespan of 7–10 days.
- **Erythrocytes (RBCs)**: Carry oxygen. They lack nuclei and have a lifespan of 120 days.
- **Mast cells**: Involved in allergic response. They have numerous purple cytoplasmic granules.
- **Granulocytes**:
  - **Neutrophils (PMNs)**: A critical part of the innate immune system that releases cytokines. They have 3–5 lobes; fewer lobes may indicate an immature band form. PMN lifespan is approximately 7 days.
  - **Basophils**: Express IgE receptors and have abundant dark blue cytoplasmic granules.
  - **Eosinophils**: Have two lobes with red granules.
- **Monocytes**: Phagocytic cells that enter tissue and become macrophages. They are large cells with a "horseshoe" nucleus and sky-blue cytoplasm.

### Lymphoid Lineage

The lymphoid lineage includes:

- **Lymphocytes**: Small, round cells with dark blue cytoplasm.
  - **Natural killer (NK) cells**: Part of the innate immune system; granulated cells that induce apoptosis when triggered by Toll-like receptors.
  - **T cells**: Involved in cell-mediated immunity. Produced in bone marrow and mature in the thymus.
  - **B cells**: Involved in humoral (antibody-driven) adaptive immunity. Produce and secrete antibodies that activate the immune system to destroy pathogens. Produced in bone marrow and mature in lymph nodes or the spleen.
