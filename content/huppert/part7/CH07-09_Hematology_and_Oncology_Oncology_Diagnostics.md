# Oncology Diagnostics

## Introduction

Diagnosis is based on biopsy-proven diagnosis most of the time—"tissue is the issue."

### Exceptions

Hepatocellular carcinoma (HCC), renal cell carcinoma (RCC), and glioblastoma can be diagnosed without biopsy via imaging.

### General Rules of Thumb

- **Biopsy distant metastasis**: This provides the opportunity to diagnose the primary tumor type and confirm staging simultaneously.
- **More tissue is better**: This is especially important in the era of molecular testing.
- **Biopsy type**: Core biopsy is generally preferred over fine needle aspiration (FNA), except FNA is preferred for suspected head/neck carcinoma.

### Key Resource

The National Comprehensive Cancer Network (NCCN.org) has guidelines on initial workup and staging by suspected cancer site. Trainees can register for a free account.
