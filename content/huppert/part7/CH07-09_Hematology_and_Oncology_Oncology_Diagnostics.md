## 1. Introduction

Diagnosis is based on biopsy-proven diagnosis most of the time because "tissue is the issue." Exceptions include hepatocellular carcinoma (HCC), renal cell carcinoma (RCC), and glioblastoma, which can be diagnosed without biopsy via imaging.

**General Rules of Thumb**: Biopsy distant metastasis to provide the opportunity to diagnose the primary tumor type and confirm staging simultaneously. More tissue is better, especially in the era of molecular testing. Core biopsy is generally preferred over fine needle aspiration (FNA), except FNA is preferred for suspected head/neck carcinoma.

**Key Resource**: The National Comprehensive Cancer Network (NCCN.org) has guidelines on initial workup and staging by suspected cancer site. Trainees can register for a free account.
