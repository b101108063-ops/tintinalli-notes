# Oncology Clinical Trials

## Breast Cancer

### Extending Aromatase-Inhibitor Adjuvant Therapy to 10 Years

**Reference**: N Engl J Med. 2016;375:209–219.

- Multicenter, double-blind, randomized placebo-controlled phase 3 clinical trial that randomized women with hormone-receptor positive early breast cancer to 5 years of the aromatase inhibitor letrozole plus 5 years of placebo versus 10 years of letrozole.
- Adjuvant aromatase inhibitor treatment for 10 years resulted in significantly increased rates of disease-free survival and a lower incidence of contralateral breast cancer than those with placebo, but did not change overall survival.

### Palbociclib and Letrozole in Advanced Breast Cancer (PALOMA-2 Trial)

**Reference**: N Engl J Med. 2016;375(20):1925–1936.

- Double-blind randomized phase 2 clinical trial that randomized 666 postmenopausal women with ER-positive, HER2-negative breast cancer, who had no prior treatment for advanced disease, to receive palbociclib (a small molecule cyclin-dependent kinase inhibitor) plus letrozole or placebo plus letrozole.
- The median progression-free survival was 24.8 months in the palbociclib–letrozole group, as compared with 14.5 months in the placebo–letrozole group.

## Colon Cancer

### MOSAIC Trial: Improved Overall Survival with Oxaliplatin, Fluorouracil, and Leucovorin

**Reference**: J Clin Oncol. 2009;27(19):3109–3116.

- Multicenter, randomized phase 3 clinical trial that randomized patients with stage II and III colon cancer to receive adjuvant 5FU + leucovorin (LV5FU2) versus 5FU + leucovorin + oxaliplatin (FOLFOX4).
- Five-year disease-free survival (DFS) rates were 73.3% and 67.4% in the FOLFOX4 and LV5FU2 groups, respectively. The addition of oxaliplatin to 5FU as adjuvant therapy also improved survival.

## Esophageal Cancer

### CROSS Trial: Preoperative Chemoradiotherapy for Esophageal or Junctional Cancer

**Reference**: N Engl J Med. 2012;366(22):2074–2084.

- Multicenter randomized phase 3 clinical trial that randomized patients with resectable tumors to receive surgery alone or preoperative concurrent chemoradiation with weekly carboplatin/paclitaxel followed by surgery.
- Median overall survival was 49.4 months in the chemoradiation + surgery group versus 24.0 months in the surgery alone group.

## Lung Cancer

### Durvalumab after Chemoradiotherapy in Stage III NSCLC (PACIFIC Trial)

**Reference**: N Engl J Med. 2017;377(20):1919–1929.

- Multicenter randomized phase 3 clinical trial that randomized patients with stage III non–small cell lung cancer (NSCLC) who underwent definitive chemoradiation to receive 1 year of adjuvant therapy with the checkpoint inhibitor durvalumab versus placebo.
- The addition of 1 year of adjuvant durvalumab improved progression-free survival compared to placebo (16.8 versus 5.6 months).

### Pembrolizumab versus Chemotherapy for PDL1-Positive NSCLC (KEYNOTE-024 Trial)

**Reference**: N Engl J Med. 2016;375:1823–1833.

- Multicenter, open-label phase 3 clinical trial that randomized patients with advanced NSCLC and PDL1 expression of at least 50% of tumor cells and no sensitizing mutation of EGFR or ALK translocation to receive either pembrolizumab (at a fixed dose of 200 mg every 3 weeks) or the investigator's choice of platinum-based chemotherapy.
- Pembrolizumab treatment improved progression-free survival (10.3 versus 6.0 months) and was associated with fewer adverse events compared to platinum-based chemotherapy.

### Osimertinib or Platinum–Pemetrexed in EGFR T790M–Positive Lung Cancer (AURA3 Trial)

**Reference**: N Engl J Med. 2017;376(7):629–640.

- Randomized, open-labeled, international phase 3 clinical trial that assigned 419 patients with T790M-positive advanced NSCLC, who had disease progression after first-line EGFR-TKI therapy, to receive either the third-generation EGFR-tyrosine kinase inhibitor osimertinib (80 mg PO daily) or chemotherapy with IV pemetrexed and either carboplatin or cisplatin q3 weeks for up to six cycles.
- The median duration of progression-free survival was significantly longer with osimertinib than with pemetrexed plus platinum chemotherapy (10.1 months versus 4.4 months, respectively).

## Prostate Cancer

### Chemohormonal Therapy in Metastatic Hormone-Sensitive Prostate Cancer (CHAARTED Trial)

**Reference**: N Engl J Med. 2015;373(8):737–746.

- Multicenter randomized phase 3 clinical trial that randomized patients with metastatic, hormone-sensitive prostate cancer to receive either androgen deprivation therapy (ADT) plus six cycles of docetaxel or ADT alone.
- At a median follow-up of 28.9 months, the median overall survival (OS) was 13.6 months longer with ADT plus docetaxel versus ADT alone (57.6 months versus 44.0 months).

### Abiraterone for Prostate Cancer Not Previously Treated with Hormone Therapy (LATITUDE Trial)

**Reference**: N Engl J Med. 2017;377(4):338–351.

- Multicenter randomized phase 3 clinical trial that randomized men with locally advanced or metastatic prostate cancer to receive ADT plus abiraterone and prednisolone versus ADT alone.
- The addition of abiraterone was associated with significantly higher rates of overall and failure-free survival than ADT alone.
