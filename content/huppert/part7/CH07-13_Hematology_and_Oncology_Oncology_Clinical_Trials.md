## 1. Breast Cancer

**MA.27 Trial (N Engl J Med 2016;375:209–219)**: This was a multicenter, double-blind, randomized placebo-controlled phase 3 clinical trial that randomized women with hormone-receptor positive early breast cancer to 5 years of the aromatase inhibitor letrozole plus 5 years of placebo versus 10 years of letrozole. Adjuvant aromatase inhibitor treatment for 10 years resulted in significantly increased rates of disease-free survival and a lower incidence of contralateral breast cancer than those with placebo, but did not change overall survival.

**PALOMA-2 Trial (N Engl J Med 2016;375:1925–1936)**: This was a double-blind randomized phase 2 clinical trial that randomized 666 postmenopausal women with ER-positive, HER2-negative breast cancer who had no prior treatment for advanced disease to receive palbociclib (a small molecule cyclin-dependent kinase inhibitor) plus letrozole or placebo plus letrozole. The median progression-free survival was 24.8 months in the palbociclib–letrozole group, as compared with 14.5 months in the placebo–letrozole group.

## 2. Colon Cancer

**MOSAIC Trial (J Clin Onc 2009;27:3109–3116)**: This was a multicenter, randomized phase 3 clinical trial that randomized patients with stage II and III colon cancer to receive adjuvant 5FU + leucovorin (LV5FU2) versus 5FU+leucovorin+oxaliplatin (FOLFOX4). Five-year disease-free survival rates were **73.3%** and **67.4%** in the FOLFOX4 and LV5FU2 groups, respectively, and the addition of oxaliplatin to 5FU as adjuvant therapy also improved survival.

## 3. Esophageal Cancer

**CROSS Trial (N Engl J Med 2012;366:2074–2084)**: This was a multicenter randomized phase 3 clinical trial that randomized patients with resectable tumors to receive surgery alone or preoperative concurrent chemoradiation with weekly carboplatin/paclitaxel followed by surgery. Median overall survival was 49.4 months in the chemoradiation plus surgery group versus 24.0 months in the surgery alone group.

## 4. Lung Cancer

**PACIFIC Trial (N Engl J Med 2017;377:1919–1929)**: This was a multicenter randomized phase 3 clinical trial that randomized patients with stage III non–small cell lung cancer (NSCLC) who underwent definitive chemoradiation to receive 1 year of adjuvant therapy with the checkpoint inhibitor durvalumab versus placebo. The addition of 1 year of adjuvant durvalumab improved progression-free survival compared to placebo (16.8 versus 5.6 months).

**KEYNOTE-024 Trial (N Engl J Med 2016;375:1823–1833)**: This was a multicenter, open-label phase 3 clinical trial that randomized patients with advanced NSCLC and PDL1 expression of at least **50%** of tumor cells and no sensitizing mutation of EGFR or ALK translocation to receive either pembrolizumab or the investigator's choice of platinum-based chemotherapy. Pembrolizumab treatment improved progression-free survival (10.3 versus 6.0 months) and was associated with fewer adverse events compared to platinum-based chemotherapy.

**AURA3 Trial (N Engl J Med 2017;376:629–640)**: This was a randomized, open-labeled, international phase 3 clinical trial that assigned 419 patients with T790M-positive advanced NSCLC who had disease progression after first-line EGFR-TKI therapy to receive either the third-generation EGFR-tyrosine kinase inhibitor osimertinib (80 mg PO daily) or chemotherapy with IV pemetrexed and either carboplatin or cisplatin every 3 weeks for up to six cycles. The median duration of progression-free survival was significantly longer with osimertinib than with pemetrexed plus platinum chemotherapy (10.1 months versus 4.4 months, respectively).

## 5. Prostate Cancer

**CHAARTED Trial (N Engl J Med 2015;373:737–746)**: This was a multicenter randomized phase 3 clinical trial that randomized patients with metastatic, hormone-sensitive prostate cancer to receive either androgen deprivation therapy (ADT) plus six cycles of docetaxel or ADT alone. At a median follow-up of 28.9 months, the median overall survival (OS) was 13.6 months longer with ADT plus docetaxel versus ADT alone (57.6 months versus 44.0 months).

**LATITUDE Trial (N Engl J Med 2017;377:338–351)**: This was a multicenter randomized phase 3 clinical trial that randomized men with locally advanced or metastatic prostate cancer to receive ADT plus abiraterone and prednisolone versus ADT alone. The addition of abiraterone was associated with significantly higher rates of overall and failure-free survival than ADT alone.
