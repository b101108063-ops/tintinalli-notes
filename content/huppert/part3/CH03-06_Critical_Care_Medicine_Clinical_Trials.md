# Clinical Trials

## Shock Management

### SMART Trial

*Balanced crystalloids versus saline in critically ill patients.*
New Engl J Med 2018; 378(9):829–839.

Multicenter, cluster-randomized, multiple crossover trial that randomized over 15,000 critically ill adult patients to receive saline (0.9% sodium chloride) or balanced crystalloids (Lactated Ringer's solution or PlasmaLyte A). Use of balanced crystalloids resulted in a lower rate of the composite outcome of death from any cause, new renal replacement therapy, or persistent renal dysfunction.

### Rivers Trial

*Early goal-directed therapy for severe sepsis and septic shock.*
New Engl J Med 2001; 345(19):1368–1377.

Single-center, parallel-group, randomized controlled trial that randomized 263 patients with severe sepsis or septic shock to early goal-directed therapy or standard care. Early goal-directed therapy included several goals in the ER prior to ICU admission: CVP 8–12 mmHg (achieved with fluid boluses), MAP >65 mmHg (achieved with vasopressors if necessary), ScvO₂ >70% (achieved with pRBC transfusions and dobutamine if necessary), and urine output >0.5 mL/kg/hour. This study showed mortality benefit vs. standard therapy. More recent studies have not shown this protocolized approach to be better than standard of care, likely because standard care now includes many components of this process. However, targeting CVP 8–12 mmHg and transfusing pRBC for low ScvO₂ is no longer routine care.

## Acute Respiratory Distress Syndrome (ARDS)

### Berlin Definition

*ARDS: The Berlin Definition.*
JAMA 2012; 307(23):2526–2533.

This paper defines the ARDS Berlin criteria based on a consensus process by a panel of experts convened in 2011.

### ARMA Trial

*Ventilation with lower tidal volumes for acute lung injury and the acute respiratory distress syndrome.*
New Engl J Med 2000; 342(18):1301–1308.

Multicenter randomized controlled trial that randomized patients with ARDS to ventilation with a lung-protective strategy using lower tidal volumes of 6 mL/kg of predicted body weight (PBW) (goal plateau pressure 25–30 mmHg) vs. conventional mechanical ventilation using tidal volumes of 12 mL/kg of PBW (goal plateau pressure 45–50 mmHg). Low tidal volume ventilation improved mortality and reduced ventilator-free days.

### ALVEOLI Trial

*Higher versus lower positive end-expiratory pressures in patients with the acute respiratory distress syndrome.*
New Engl J Med 2004; 351(4):327–336.

Multicenter, randomized controlled clinical trial that randomized 549 patients with ARDS to mechanical ventilation with either lower or higher PEEP levels. Among patients who received low tidal volume ventilation of 6 mL/kg PBW and an end-inspiratory plateau pressure limit of 30 cm H₂O, there were no differences in clinical outcomes whether lower or higher PEEP levels were used.

### ACURASYS Trial

*Paralysis with cisatracurium in early severe ARDS.*
New Engl J Med 2010; 363(12):1107–1116.

Multicenter, double-blinded, placebo-controlled trial that randomized 340 patients with early severe ARDS (P/F <150) to cisatracurium-induced paralysis or placebo for 48 hours. Paralysis within 48 hours of severe ARDS onset was associated with improved mortality.

### FACTT Trial

*Fluid loop management with acute lung injury and the acute respiratory distress syndrome.*
New Engl J Med 2006; 354(24):2564–2575.

Multicenter, two-by-two factorial, randomized controlled trial that randomized 1000 patients with ALI/ARDS to a conservative fluid management strategy (targeting CVP <4 mmHg) vs. a liberal fluid management strategy (targeting CVP 10–14 mmHg). The conservative fluid strategy improved lung function, decreased ventilator days, and decreased ICU days compared to the liberal strategy, although there was no difference in mortality.

### PROSEVA Trial

*Prone positioning in severe acute respiratory distress syndrome.*
New Engl J Med 2013; 368(23):2159–2168.

Multicenter, randomized, open-label trial that randomized 466 patients with severe ARDS to early (<36 hours after intubation) or lengthy (goal 16 hours/day) intermittent prone positioning vs. a standard supine position. In patients with severe ARDS, early and prolonged proning was associated with improved 28-day mortality.
