## 3. Organizational Schema for Patient Care

| Phase | Activities |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Pre-rounding** | Review overnight events, nursing updates, patient subjective report, vital signs, physical exam, labs, new results |
| **Rounding** | Present patient in SOAP format, discuss plan by problem |
| **Post-rounding** | Place orders, communicate with consulting services, update family |
| **Daily reassessment** | Update problem list, reassess therapeutic endpoints, adjust plan as needed |
| **Discharge planning** | Medication reconciliation, follow-up appointments, patient education, discharge summary |

---

## 4. Prerounding Checklist

| Item | Details |
| :--- | :--- |
| Overnight events | Any changes in clinical status, new symptoms, procedures performed |
| Nursing updates | Input/output, concerns raised by nursing staff |
| Patient subjective | How the patient feels today, any new complaints |
| Vital signs | Current vital signs and trends |
| Physical exam | Focused exam relevant to the patient's problems |
| Labs | New or trending lab values |
| Imaging/Micro | Any new results |

## 5. Tips for Succeeding in Medical Clerkship

1. **Develop a consistent approach** to the HPI and daily presentations early in training.
2. **Know your patient** better than anyone else on the team — anticipate questions and have a plan.
3. **Communicate clearly and concisely** with attendings, residents, nurses, and patients.
4. **Be proactive** — identify problems before they become urgent and suggest solutions.
5. **Ask questions** at appropriate times and use resources to fill knowledge gaps.
6. **Prioritize tasks** — know what must be done today versus what can wait.
7. **Follow up on all results** — a good clinician follows through completely.
