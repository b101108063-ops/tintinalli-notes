## 1. Cardiovascular Disease Risk Prevention

- **FOURIER** (*N Engl J Med 2017*): 
  - *Design*: Randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled trial of 27,564 patients with atherosclerotic CVD and LDL $\ge$ **70 mg/dL** on statin therapy. Compared evolocumab (PCSK9 inhibitor, 140 mg q2wks or 420 mg monthly) vs. placebo.
  - *Result*: Evolocumab significantly reduced the risk of the composite primary end point (CV death, MI, stroke, hospitalization for unstable angina, or coronary revascularization) compared to placebo (**9.8%** vs. **11.3%**).

## 2. Coronary Artery Disease (CAD)

- **FAME-2** (*N Engl J Med 2012*): 
  - *Design*: 888 patients with stable CAD considered for PCI underwent FFR testing. Those with $\ge 1$ functionally significant lesion were randomized to FFR-guided PCI + best medical therapy vs. best medical therapy alone.
  - *Result*: FFR-guided PCI reduced the composite endpoint of death, nonfatal MI, and urgent re-catheterization compared to medical therapy alone.
- **SYNTAX** (*N Engl J Med 2009*): 
  - *Design*: 1,800 patients with previously untreated three-vessel or left main CAD randomized to CABG vs. PCI.
  - *Result*: Major adverse cardiac or cerebrovascular events at 12 months were significantly higher in the PCI group (**17.8%** vs. **12.4%** for CABG; $P = 0.002$). CABG remains the standard of care for these patients.
- **ISCHEMIA** (*N Engl J Med 2020*): 
  - *Design*: 5,179 patients with moderate to severe stable CAD randomized to conservative strategy (medical management) vs. early interventional strategy (revascularization).
  - *Result*: No significant difference in ischemic cardiovascular events or death over a median of 3.2 years.
- **ORBITA** (*The Lancet 2017*): 
  - *Design*: 230 patients with stable CAD and ischemic symptoms randomized to PCI of flow-limiting lesions vs. a placebo procedure.
  - *Result*: No difference in exercise time or symptom scores at 6 weeks follow-up.

## 3. Acute Coronary Syndrome (ACS) & Post-ACS Care

- **CAPRICORN** (*The Lancet 2001*): 
  - *Design*: 1,959 patients with hemodynamically stable MI and reduced LVEF $\le$ **40%** (already on an ACEi) randomized to carvedilol vs. placebo.
  - *Result*: Carvedilol was associated with decreased all-cause mortality (**12%** vs. **15%**).
- **MIRACL** (*JAMA 2001*): 
  - *Design*: 3,086 patients hospitalized for unstable angina/NSTEMI randomized to atorvastatin 80 mg vs. placebo daily.
  - *Result*: Early initiation of atorvastatin reduced the combined endpoint of death, nonfatal MI, cardiac arrest, and ACS requiring hospitalization at 16 weeks.
- **IMPROVE-IT** (*N Engl J Med 2015*): 
  - *Design*: 18,144 patients with recent ACS randomized to simvastatin 40 mg + ezetimibe 10 mg vs. simvastatin 40 mg alone.
  - *Result*: The combination reduced CV events (CV mortality, major CV event, or nonfatal stroke) compared to statin therapy alone.

## 4. Atrial Fibrillation

- **AFFIRM** (*N Engl J Med 2002*): 
  - *Design*: 4,060 patients with nonvalvular Afib and high risk of stroke/death randomized to rate-control (BB, CCB, digoxin) vs. rhythm-control strategies.
  - *Result*: No survival benefit between the two strategies; patients undergoing rhythm control trended toward increased mortality.
- **RACE II** (*N Engl J Med 2010*): 
  - *Design*: 614 patients with permanent Afib randomized to lenient rate control (resting HR $<$ **110 bpm**) vs. strict rate control (resting HR $<$ **80 bpm**).
  - *Result*: Lenient rate control was non-inferior to strict rate control in preventing CV events over 3 years.

## 5. Heart Failure with Reduced Ejection Fraction (HFrEF)

- **PARADIGM-HF** (*N Engl J Med 2014*): 
  - *Design*: 8,399 patients with HFrEF (LVEF $\le$ **40%**) and NYHA II–IV symptoms randomized to sacubitril/valsartan (Entresto) 200 mg PO BID vs. enalapril 10 mg PO BID.
  - *Result*: Entresto reduced CV mortality and HF hospitalizations compared to enalapril, and was associated with reduced all-cause mortality.
- **DAPA-HF** (*N Engl J Med 2019*): 
  - *Design*: 4,744 patients with HFrEF (EF $\le$ **40%**) and NYHA II-IV symptoms ($\pm$ diabetes) randomized to dapagliflozin (SGLT2 inhibitor) vs. placebo.
  - *Result*: Addition of dapagliflozin decreased rates of CV death, worsening HF, and all-cause mortality.

## 6. Heart Failure with Preserved Ejection Fraction (HFpEF)

- **TOPCAT** (*N Engl J Med 2014*): 
  - *Design*: 3,445 patients with HFpEF (LVEF $>$ **45%**, findings of HF, and either a HF hospitalization or elevated BNP) randomized to spironolactone vs. placebo.
  - *Result*: Spironolactone did not significantly reduce the primary composite endpoint, but was associated with a small reduction in HF hospitalizations. *(Note: Controversial study due to regional differences in subgroup analysis).*

## 7. Aortic Stenosis

- **PARTNER A & B** (*N Engl J Med 2010/2011*): 
  - *Design*: Evaluated transcatheter aortic valve replacement (TAVR) vs. surgical aortic valve replacement (SAVR) in severe aortic stenosis.
  - *Result*: Demonstrated that TAVR is equivalent (and in some cases superior) to SAVR at decreasing death, stroke, and re-hospitalization in severe aortic stenosis.
