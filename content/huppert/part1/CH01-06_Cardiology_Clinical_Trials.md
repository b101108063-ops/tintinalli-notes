## 1. Cardiovascular Disease Risk Prevention

### FOURIER (*N Engl J Med 2017*)

- **Full title**: Further Cardiovascular Outcomes Research with PCSK9 Inhibition in Subjects with Elevated Risk
- **Study design**: Randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled trial.
- **Population**: 27,564 patients with atherosclerotic cardiovascular disease and LDL $\ge 70$ mg/dL on statin therapy.
- **Intervention**: Evolocumab (PCSK9 inhibitor, 140 mg every 2 weeks or 420 mg monthly) versus placebo.
- **Result**: Evolocumab significantly reduced the risk of the composite primary endpoint (cardiovascular death, myocardial infarction, stroke, hospitalization for unstable angina, or coronary revascularization) compared to placebo (**9.8% vs. 11.3%**).

## 2. Coronary Artery Disease (CAD)

### FAME-2 (*N Engl J Med 2012*)

- **Full title**: Fractional Flow Reserve versus Angiography for Multivessel Evaluation
- **Study design**: 888 patients with stable CAD considered for PCI underwent fractional flow reserve (FFR) testing. Those with $\ge 1$ functionally significant lesion were randomized.
- **Intervention**: FFR-guided PCI plus best medical therapy versus best medical therapy alone.
- **Result**: FFR-guided PCI reduced the composite endpoint of death, nonfatal myocardial infarction, and urgent re-catheterization compared to medical therapy alone.

### SYNTAX (*N Engl J Med 2009*)

- **Full title**: Synergy between PCI with Taxus and Cardiac Surgery
- **Study design**: 1,800 patients with previously untreated three-vessel or left main CAD were randomized to CABG versus PCI.
- **Result**: Major adverse cardiac or cerebrovascular events at 12 months were significantly higher in the PCI group (**17.8%** vs. **12.4%** for CABG; $P = 0.002$). CABG remains the standard of care for these patients.

### ISCHEMIA (*N Engl J Med 2020*)

- **Full title**: International Study of Comparative Health Effectiveness with Medical and Invasive Approaches
- **Study design**: 5,179 patients with moderate to severe stable CAD were randomized to a conservative strategy (medical management) versus an early interventional strategy (revascularization).
- **Result**: No significant difference in ischemic cardiovascular events or death over a median follow-up of 3.2 years.

### ORBITA (*The Lancet 2017*)

- **Full title**: Coronary Balloon Angioplasty with or without Stent Insertion in Stable Angina
- **Study design**: 230 patients with stable CAD and ischemic symptoms were randomized to PCI of flow-limiting lesions versus a placebo (sham) procedure.
- **Result**: No difference in exercise time or symptom scores at 6 weeks follow-up.

## 3. Acute Coronary Syndrome (ACS) & Post-ACS Care

### CAPRICORN (*The Lancet 2001*)

- **Full title**: Carvedilol Post-Infarction Survival Control in Left Ventricular Dysfunction
- **Study design**: 1,959 patients with hemodynamically stable myocardial infarction and reduced LVEF $\le 40\%$ (already on an ACE inhibitor) were randomized to carvedilol versus placebo.
- **Result**: Carvedilol was associated with decreased all-cause mortality (**12% vs. 15%**).

### MIRACL (*JAMA 2001*)

- **Full title**: Myocardial Ischemia Reduction with Aggressive Cholesterol Lowering
- **Study design**: 3,086 patients hospitalized for unstable angina or NSTEMI were randomized to atorvastatin 80 mg versus placebo daily.
- **Result**: Early initiation of atorvastatin reduced the combined endpoint of death, nonfatal myocardial infarction, cardiac arrest, and ACS requiring hospitalization at 16 weeks.

### IMPROVE-IT (*N Engl J Med 2015*)

- **Full title**: Improved Reduction of Outcomes: Vytorin Efficacy International Trial
- **Study design**: 18,144 patients with recent ACS were randomized to simvastatin 40 mg plus ezetimibe 10 mg versus simvastatin 40 mg alone.
- **Result**: The combination therapy reduced cardiovascular events (cardiovascular mortality, major cardiovascular event, or nonfatal stroke) compared to statin monotherapy.

## 4. Atrial Fibrillation

### AFFIRM (*N Engl J Med 2002*)

- **Full title**: Atrial Fibrillation Follow-up Investigation of Rhythm Management
- **Study design**: 4,060 patients with nonvalvular atrial fibrillation and high risk of stroke or death were randomized to rate-control strategy (beta-blocker, calcium channel blocker, digoxin) versus rhythm-control strategy (antiarrhythmic drugs or ablation).
- **Result**: No survival benefit was observed between the two strategies; patients undergoing rhythm control trended toward increased mortality.

### RACE II (*N Engl J Med 2010*)

- **Full title**: Rate Control Efficacy in Permanent Atrial Fibrillation
- **Study design**: 614 patients with permanent atrial fibrillation were randomized to lenient rate control (resting heart rate $< 110$ bpm) versus strict rate control (resting heart rate $< 80$ bpm).
- **Result**: Lenient rate control was non-inferior to strict rate control in preventing cardiovascular events over 3 years of follow-up.

## 5. Heart Failure with Reduced Ejection Fraction (HFrEF)

### PARADIGM-HF (*N Engl J Med 2014*)

- **Full title**: Prospective Comparison of ARNI with ACEI to Determine Impact on Global Mortality and Morbidity in Heart Failure
- **Study design**: 8,399 patients with HFrEF (LVEF $\le 40\%$) and NYHA class II–IV symptoms were randomized to sacubitril/valsartan (Entresto) 200 mg PO twice daily versus enalapril 10 mg PO twice daily.
- **Result**: Sacubitril/valsartan reduced cardiovascular mortality and heart failure hospitalizations compared to enalapril, and was associated with reduced all-cause mortality.

### DAPA-HF (*N Engl J Med 2019*)

- **Full title**: Dapagliflozin and Prevention of Adverse Outcomes in Heart Failure
- **Study design**: 4,744 patients with HFrEF (EF $\le 40\%$) and NYHA class II–IV symptoms (with or without diabetes) were randomized to dapagliflozin (SGLT2 inhibitor) versus placebo.
- **Result**: The addition of dapagliflozin decreased rates of cardiovascular death, worsening heart failure, and all-cause mortality.

## 6. Heart Failure with Preserved Ejection Fraction (HFpEF)

### TOPCAT (*N Engl J Med 2014*)

- **Full title**: Treatment of Preserved Cardiac Function Heart Failure with an Aldosterone Antagonist
- **Study design**: 3,445 patients with HFpEF (LVEF $> 45\%$, clinical findings of heart failure, and either a heart failure hospitalization or elevated BNP) were randomized to spironolactone versus placebo.
- **Result**: Spironolactone did not significantly reduce the primary composite endpoint. However, it was associated with a small reduction in heart failure hospitalizations.
  - *Note*: This study is controversial due to regional differences observed in subgroup analysis (patients from Eastern Europe had markedly different outcomes compared to those from North/South America).

## 7. Aortic Stenosis

### PARTNER A & B (*N Engl J Med 2010/2011*)

- **Full title**: Placement of Aortic Transcatheter Valves (PARTNER) trials
- **Study design**: Two parallel trials evaluated transcatheter aortic valve replacement (TAVR) versus surgical aortic valve replacement (SAVR) in patients with severe aortic stenosis:
  - **PARTNER A**: Inoperable or high-risk surgical patients
  - **PARTNER B**: Operable patients
- **Result**: TAVR was demonstrated to be equivalent (and in some patient subgroups, superior) to SAVR at decreasing death, stroke, and re-hospitalization in severe aortic stenosis. These landmark trials established TAVR as a viable alternative to open surgery for appropriate candidates.
