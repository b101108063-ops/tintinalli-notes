## Thoracentesis

### Indications

1. Diagnostic for pleural effusion of unknown cause
2. Therapeutic for respiratory symptoms due to large effusions

### Tests to Consider

- Pleural fluid protein and LDH (for Light's criteria)
- Cell count and differential (PMN predominance suggests acute process such as parapneumonic effusion or PE; mononuclear predominance suggests cancer or TB)
- Gram stain and culture
- Hct (1–20% suggests cancer, PE, trauma; >50% suggests hemothorax)
- Glucose (<60 mg/dL suggests parapneumonic effusion or empyema, cancer, TB, RA, SLE, or esophageal rupture)
- Cytology
- pH (helpful for diagnosing parapneumonic effusion or empyema; pH <7.2 in most cases)
- Triglycerides (>100 mg/dL suggests chylothorax)
- Amylase (elevated if pleural effusion due to pancreatic disease or esophageal rupture)

### Relative Contraindications

1. Coagulopathy/thrombocytopenia (especially DIC)
2. Overlying cellulitis
3. Inability to tolerate a potential complication (e.g., patient with only one functional lung)

### Complications

- **Pneumothorax:** Rare; obtain post-procedure CXR if air was aspirated, patient develops chest pain/dyspnea/hypoxia, multiple needle passes, or patient is critically ill/on mechanical ventilation
- **Minor:** Pain, coughing, localized infection
- **Severe:** Hemothorax, intra-abdominal organ injury, air embolism, postexpansion pulmonary edema (avoid by limiting therapeutic aspirations to <1500 mL)

## Chest Tubes

### Description

Flexible plastic tube placed into the pleural space (or mediastinum) through the chest wall for drainage of air, fluid, or pus.

### Indications

- **Air:** Tension pneumothorax
- **Fluid:** Hemothorax, persistent effusion (do NOT put in for hepatohydrothorax)
- **Pus:** Empyema

### Components (Three-Bottle System)

1. **Collection trap:** Tubes on right side
2. **Water seal:** Gradient to flow out; look for air leak in form of bubbles
3. **Suction to wall:** With suction regulator; normal to see bubbles

### Parameters to Report on Rounds

- **Suction:** Is the tube on water seal or suction? If on suction, at what pressure (cm H₂O)?
- **Volume:** How much has the chest tube drained? What is the rate (increasing/decreasing)?
- **Quality:** What color is the fluid?
- **Air leak:** Is there an air leak?
