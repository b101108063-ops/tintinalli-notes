# Diagnostics

## Chest X-Ray (CXR)

### Types of CXR

| Type | Method | Indications |
|------|--------|-------------|
| PA and Lateral CXR | Radiation travels posterior → anterior; obtained with lateral images | Most common modality for lung imaging |
| AP Portable CXR | Radiation travels anterior → posterior | Used for unstable or hospitalized patients; does not provide as much information as PA CXR; subject to patient rotation issues; no lateral images; enlarges cardiac silhouette |
| Lateral Decubitus CXR | Patient placed in lateral decubitus position (on side) | Assessment of free-flowing vs. loculated pleural fluid; increasingly replaced by point-of-care ultrasound (POCUS), especially for assessment of effusions |

### Approach to CXR Interpretation

1. **Verify** patient name and date
2. **Identify** CXR type
3. **Evaluate quality:**
   - **Rotation:** Clavicular symmetry; trachea midline
   - **Inspiration:** Count for 8.5–11 rib spaces above the diaphragm
   - **Penetration:** If thoracic spine is seen through heart, the film is overexposed
4. **Read systematically:**
   - Airway (trachea)
   - Bronchoalveolar markings
   - Cardiac silhouette (and assessment of mediastinum)
   - Diaphragm
   - Extras/Everything Else (lung parenchyma, MSK, lines/tubes)

**CXR Patterns:** Patterns can be described as linear, reticular, nodular, or reticulonodular.

## Chest CT

### Types of Chest CT

| Type | Description | Indications |
|------|-------------|-------------|
| Standard Chest CT | May be obtained with or without contrast | Malignancy workup; evaluation for chest wall/mediastinum infection |
| High-Resolution CT (HRCT) | Very thin cuts with outstanding spatial resolution | Detection of interstitial lung disease, particularly honeycombing (can be missed on standard CT); expiratory views useful to detect air trapping (e.g., hypersensitivity pneumonitis) |
| CT Angiography | Image with contrast; timing optimized | Detection of specific diagnoses such as pulmonary emboli |
| Low-Dose CT (LDCT) | Reduced radiation | Lung cancer screening (National Lung Cancer Screening Trial, NEJM 2011) |

### Interpreting Chest CT Images

**Densities:**
- **Dense** = bright/white
- **Air** = black
- **Fluid/Soft tissue** = gray
- **Blood** = white
- **Bone/Contrast** = dense white

**Windows adjust displayed densities:**
- **Lung window:** Lung, airways, lung parenchyma
- **Soft tissue window:** Mediastinal structures, heart, thyroid, muscles, lymph nodes
- **Bone window:** Bone

### Approach to Chest CT Interpretation

1. **Verify** patient name and date
2. **Identify** chest CT type, including whether contrast was administered
3. **Select appropriate window** (often start with lung, then use soft tissue)
4. **Read systematically:**
   - Air: Airway, lung parenchyma
   - Bone: Evaluate for fractures, metastases
   - Cardiac/vessels: Assess anatomy, clots, false lumen
   - Digestive: Evaluate esophagus patency
   - Extras: Tubes, foreign bodies
   - Soft tissue: Muscles, fat

## Differential Diagnosis Based on Radiographic Findings

| Finding | Differential Diagnosis |
|---------|----------------------|
| **Segmental/Lobar Consolidation** | Common bacterial pathogens (including Legionella); less common: TB, Nocardia, fungal |
| **Solitary Nodule** | **Benign (>99%):** Infectious (endemic fungi: histoplasmosis, coccidioidomycosis; mycobacteria: TB, NTM; less commonly abscess-forming bacteria such as S. aureus); Tumor (hamartoma); Pulmonary AV malformation; Other (inflammatory lesions: RA, GPA, sarcoidosis, amyloidosis; rounded atelectasis). **Malignancy (<1%):** Primary lung cancer; Metastatic cancer (especially melanoma, sarcoma, bronchus, colon, breast, kidney, testicle) |
| **Multiple Nodules** | **Centrilobular:** Infection and aspiration (bacterial, fungal, mycobacterial, NTM); Hypersensitivity pneumonitis (HP); Respiratory bronchiolitis (RB); Endobronchial tumor. **Perilymphatic:** Inflammatory (sarcoidosis, pneumoconiosis, LIP); Malignancy (lymphangitic carcinomatosis, leukemia/lymphoma); Amyloidosis. **Random distribution:** Hematogenous infection (septic emboli, disseminated fungal, miliary TB); Hematogenous malignancy; Langerhans cell histiocytosis (LCH, with cysts) |
| **Cavitation** | Lung abscess and necrotizing pneumonia (anaerobes most common, MRSA, Pseudomonas); Atypicals (TB/NTM including MAC, Nocardia); Fungal (yeasts: Cryptococcus; dimorphic endemics: Histoplasma, Coccidioides; molds: Aspergillus, Zygomycetes); Septic emboli; Malignancy (primary lung cancers, lymphoma, Kaposi's sarcoma, metastatic disease); Rheumatologic (granulomatosis with polyangiitis; less commonly RA and sarcoidosis); PE; Other (bronchiolitis obliterans with organizing pneumonia, LCH, amyloidosis) |
| **Ground Glass Opacities (GGOs)** | Most common: Edema (cardiogenic or noncardiogenic). Infectious: Viral (respiratory viruses, influenza, RSV; CMV); Atypicals (rarely Mycoplasma, Chlamydia, Q fever, leptospirosis); Pneumocystis pneumonia (PCP); Parasitic and fungal including Toxoplasma and Strongyloides. Hemorrhage including diffuse alveolar hemorrhage (DAH). Others: Drug-induced ALI; Cystic (LAM, LCH with nodules, LIP); Fibrotic (DPLD from known cause or idiopathic, granulomatous DPLD) |

## Pulmonary Function Testing (PFTs)

### Approach to Interpreting PFTs

**Key Questions:**
1. Is there obstruction?
2. Is there restriction?
3. Is there pathology disruptive to the alveolar–capillary membrane?

**Method:**
- Assess flow–volume loop: curvilinear = obstructive; steep/vertical = restrictive
- Evaluate FEV1/FVC: <0.7 = obstructive
- If obstructive: assess reversibility and DLCO to determine etiology
- Evaluate TLC: <80% predicted = restrictive
- If restrictive: assess DLCO to determine etiology
- Evaluate DLCO
- Consider methacholine challenge (asthma may have baseline normal FEV1/FVC, TLC, DLCO)

**Flow–Volume Loops:**
- In obstructive disease, the flow–volume loop becomes curvilinear.
- In restrictive disease, the flow–volume loop becomes steep/vertical.

**Abbreviations:** PFT = pulmonary function test; FEV1 = forced expiratory volume in one second; FVC = forced vital capacity; DLCO = diffusion capacity for carbon monoxide; ILD = interstitial lung disease; NM = neuromuscular disorder; Hct = hematocrit
