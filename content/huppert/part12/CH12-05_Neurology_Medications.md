## 2. Stroke

### NINDS Trial

**Tissue Plasminogen Activator for Acute Ischemic Stroke**. N Engl J Med 1995;333:1581–1588.

**Design**: Double-blinded, randomized controlled trial that randomized 624 patients to receive tissue plasminogen activator (tPA) vs. placebo within 3 hours of an ischemic stroke.

**Results**: This trial was the first to establish the efficacy of thrombolysis within 3 hours of an ischemic stroke, demonstrating improvements in functional outcomes at 3 months. There was a tenfold increase in symptomatic intracranial hemorrhage (ICH) with tPA administration, but no significant difference in mortality.

### ECASS III

**European Cooperative Acute Stroke Study: Thrombolysis with Alteplase 3 to 4.5 Hours After Acute Ischemic Stroke (ECASS III)**. N Engl J Med 2008;359:1317–1329.

**Design**: Multicenter, double-blinded, randomized placebo-controlled trial that randomized 821 patients to receive tPA or placebo within 4.5 hours of the onset of ischemic stroke symptoms.

**Results**: Treatment with tPA at 3–4.5 hours from stroke onset was associated with more favorable outcomes and more symptomatic ICH compared to placebo, extending the window of tPA administration to 4.5 hours.

### MR CLEAN

**Multicenter Randomized Clinical Trial of Endovascular Treatment for Acute Ischemic Stroke in the Netherlands (MR CLEAN)**. N Engl J Med 2008;372:11–20.

**Design**: Multicenter open-label randomized controlled trial that randomized 500 patients with acute ischemic stroke and imaging proven proximal anterior intracranial circulation large vessel occlusion to receive usual care or usual care plus intra-arterial therapy within 6 hours.

**Results**: In patients with large proximal anterior circulation strokes, usual care plus endovascular therapy was both safe and superior to usual care at 90 days.

### NASCET

**Beneficial Effect of Carotid Endarterectomy In Symptomatic Patients with High-Grade Stenosis (NASCET)**. N Engl J Med 1991;325(7):445–453.

**Design**: Multicenter parallel group randomized controlled trial that randomized over 2000 patients with symptomatic cervical ICA stenosis to undergo carotid endarterectomy (CEA) versus medical therapy.

**Results**: CEA reduced the 5-year risk of death or stroke by **29%** in patients with 50–**69%** stenosis. Patients with ≥**70%** stenosis received such a dramatic benefit that this study arm was closed prematurely and all patients with severe stenosis were referred for CEA.

### CAST

**Randomized Placebo-Controlled Trial of Early Aspirin Use in 20,000 Patients with Acute Ischemic Stroke (CAST)**. Lancet 1997;349(9066):1641–1649.

**Design**: Multicenter randomized placebo controlled trial that randomized patients with suspected acute ischemic stroke to receive aspirin 160 mg daily within 48 hours and continue for 4 weeks vs. placebo.

**Results**: Patients randomized to the aspirin arm had a small (**1%**) but statistically significant reduction in death and recurrent ischemic stroke compared to the placebo group.

### SPARCL

**High-dose Atorvastatin After Stroke or Transient Ischemic Attack (SPARCL)**. N Engl J Med 2006;355:549–559.

**Design**: Multicenter double blind placebo-controlled trial that randomized patients with a history of stroke/TIA in the preceding 1–6 months to receive atorvastatin 80 mg daily or placebo.

**Results**: Atorvastatin reduced the composite primary endpoint of fatal or nonfatal stroke by **16%**, and also reduced the secondary endpoint of fatal stroke by **43%** at 5 years compared to placebo.
