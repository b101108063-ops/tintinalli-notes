# Diagnostics

## The Neurologic Exam

### Mental Status

- **Orientation:** Assess person, place, time, and situation.
- **Language:** Evaluate fluency, repetition, naming (high and low frequency items), and comprehension.
  - Axial commands: "Close eyes, stick out tongue."
  - Appendicular commands: "Show two fingers, give thumbs up."
  - Complex commands: "Take your right thumb, touch your left ear, and stick out your tongue."
- **Attention:** Ask the patient to spell "WORLD" forward, then backward. Alternatively, recite months of the year or days of the week backward.
- **Memory:** Assess working memory (digit span forward [normal >5–7 digits], backward) and delayed memory (remember three words × 5 minutes).

### Cranial Nerves

| Nerve | Assessment |
|-------|------------|
| **I – Olfactory** | Not frequently tested |
| **II – Optic / III parasympathetic fibers** | Visual fields, pupillary constriction (afferent = optic nerve; efferent = parasympathetic fibers) |
| **III, IV, VI – Oculomotor, trochlear, abducens** | Extraocular muscle movements |
| **V – Trigeminal** | Facial sensation, motor function for muscles of mastication |
| **VII – Facial** | Facial movement, eyelid closure, lacrimation and salivation, taste in anterior 2/3 of tongue |
| **VIII – Vestibulocochlear** | Hearing (cochlear nerve), balance (vestibular nerve) |
| **IX, X – Glossopharyngeal and vagus** | Palate elevation, gag reflex, cough |

### Motor Exam

- **Bulk and tone:** Increased = spasticity, rigidity, or paratonia; decreased = flaccidity.

#### Screening Motor Exam

- **Pronator drift:** A sign of upper motor neuron weakness; evaluate for finger flexion and/or pronation with arms outstretched and eyes closed.
- **Finger taps and toe taps:** Evaluate for large, symmetric, rapid movements.

#### Confrontational Strength Testing

Score motor strength in each muscle group from 0 to 5:

| Grade | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| **0** | No contraction |
| **1** | Trace movement |
| **2** | Full range of motion when gravity is eliminated |
| **3** | Full range of motion against gravity |
| **4** | Movement against gravity with resistance, but still less than full strength |
| **5** | Full/normal strength |

#### Preferential Muscle Groups to Test

- **Upper extremity:** Shoulder abduction (deltoids), flexion and extension at elbow (biceps, triceps), wrist extension, finger extension and abduction.
- **Lower extremity:** Hip flexion (iliopsoas), knee flexion and extension (hamstrings, quadriceps), ankle dorsiflexion (tibialis anterior).

### Sensory Exam

#### Screening Exam

- **Small fiber:** Pain (safety pin) and temperature (alcohol swab, cold tuning fork).
- **Large fiber:** Vibration (tuning fork) and proprioception (joint position sense).

### Reflexes

- **Upper extremity reflexes:** Biceps (C5–6), triceps (C7–8).
- **Lower extremity reflexes:** Patellae (L3–4), Achilles tendon (S1–S2).
- **Plantar response:** Apply noxious stimulus starting at the lateral aspect of the sole of the foot, moving medially across the ball of the foot.
  - **Normal response:** Flexor (toes down).
  - **Abnormal response (Babinski sign):** Extensor — large toe goes up and remaining toes fan out.

### Coordination

- **Appendicular (limb) ataxia:** Finger-to-nose-to-finger, heel-to-knee-to-shin.
- **Truncal ataxia:** Sitting upright unsupported; gait ataxia.

### Gait

- **Description of gait:** Assess stance (normal, wide, or narrow-based), stride length, foot clearance (shuffling; foot drop → steppage gait), and arm swing (decreased in parkinsonism).

#### Romberg Test

- Balance requires visual, vestibular (inner ear), and proprioceptive (positional sense) systems.
- **Important:** Romberg testing eliminates vision and tests the remaining two systems. This is NOT a test of cerebellar function.

## Imaging

### Noncontrast Head Computed Tomography (NCHCT)

- **Description:** CT scanning is particularly useful for evaluation of bony defects, metallic objects, calcifications, hemorrhage, and hydrocephalus.

- **Common indications:** Acute stroke (to exclude hemorrhage), tumor, hydrocephalus, trauma, subarachnoid hemorrhage, and bony or sinus disease.

- **Pitfalls:** Insensitive for early ischemic stroke changes and posterior fossa and spinal cord lesions, among others.

### Magnetic Resonance Imaging (MRI)

- **Description:** MRI does not involve ionizing radiation and provides superior soft tissue sensitivity.

- **Common indications:** Stroke, tumor, parenchymal pathology (ischemia, demyelinating lesions, edema, infection, etc.) of the brain or spinal cord.

- **Sequences:**
  - **T1:** Gray matter is gray, white matter is white, CSF is black.
  - **T2:** Gray matter is white, white matter is gray, CSF is white.
  - **T2/FLAIR:** Same as T2 except CSF is black.

- **Clinical Pearl:** T1 is usually helpful for anatomy only. On T2, because CSF is white (water), edema is also white, which means pathology appears bright and is typically easier to see. T2/FLAIR subtracts the bright CSF signal to make pathology even easier to appreciate.

## Lumbar Puncture

### Common Indications

- Concern for CNS infection, autoimmune or inflammatory condition, or malignancy.
- Can also be used to diagnose subarachnoid hemorrhage, intracranial pressure abnormalities, or to reduce CSF pressure therapeutically.

### Obtain NCHCT Prior to LP If

- Patient is greater than 60 years old.
- Immunocompromised state.
- History of CNS disease (e.g., mass lesion, stroke, focal infection).
- Seizure within one week of presentation.
- Focal neurologic deficit.

### CSF Studies

**Always send:**
- Cell count with differential
- Protein
- Glucose (check serum glucose simultaneously)

**Additional CSF testing to consider based on clinical suspicion:**

| Category | Tests |
|----------|-------|
| **Autoimmune or inflammatory** | Oligoclonal bands, IgG index, targeted autoantibody tests |
| **Infection** | Bacterial gram stain and culture, targeted infectious studies (PCR, serology, AFB, fungal, or viral culture) |
| **Malignancy** | Cytology and flow cytometry |

### Contraindications

- **Local infection** over the site of puncture.
- **Coagulopathy:** Platelet goal >50,000/μL, INR < 1.7.
- If a known intracranial or spinal cord mass lesion is present, always obtain imaging first. Mass lesions are not always a contraindication for LP, but imaging should be reviewed prior to attempted LP.

## Electroencephalogram (EEG)

- **Definition:** A test used to evaluate the electrical activity in the brain, particularly for seizure or "interictal" (between seizures) epileptiform discharges.

- **Indications:**
  - Evaluation of suspected epilepsy and classification of seizure disorders.
  - Management of status epilepticus.
  - Evaluation of altered mental status.

## Electromyography (EMG) and Nerve Conduction Studies

### Definitions

- **EMG:** Clinical study of electrical activity of muscle fibers.
- **Nerve conduction study:** Clinical study of electrical activity from nerve roots, plexus, peripheral nerves, and/or neuromuscular junction.

### Indications

- Localization of lower motor neuron disorders, including anterior horn, nerve root, plexus, peripheral nerve, neuromuscular junction, or muscle.
- Classification of peripheral nerve disorders as axonal or demyelinating.
- Prognostication of clinical course.
