## 1. Multiple Sclerosis

**Oral versus Intravenous Steroids for Treatment of Relapses in Multiple Sclerosis**  
*Cochrane Database of Systematic Reviews 2012;(3):CD006921.*

This meta-analysis reviewed 167 randomized or quasi-randomized trials comparing oral and intravenous corticosteroids for MS relapses. The trials support the hypothesis that oral corticosteroids are equally efficacious to intravenous corticosteroids for treating acute MS exacerbations.

**A Randomized, Controlled Trial of Corticosteroids in the Treatment of Acute Optic Neuritis**  
*N Engl J Med 1992;326:581–588.*

This multicenter randomized placebo-controlled trial randomized patients with acute optic neuritis to intravenous methylprednisolone followed by oral prednisone versus placebo. High-dose intravenous methylprednisolone followed by oral prednisone accelerated visual recovery but did not improve long-term visual outcome at 6 months compared to placebo.

## 2. Neurocritical Care

**Mild Therapeutic Hypothermia to Improve the Neurologic Outcome After Cardiac Arrest**  
*N Engl J Med 2002;346(8):549–556.*

This multicenter unblinded randomized controlled trial randomized patients with return of spontaneous circulation after cardiac arrest to therapeutic hypothermia versus standard care. Patients were cooled to 32–34°C for 12–24 hours. Patients who underwent therapeutic hypothermia at 32–34°C had improved neurologic outcomes and survival at 6 months compared to standard care.

## 3. Neuromuscular Disorders

**Intravenous Immunoglobulin for Guillain-Barré Syndrome**  
*Cochrane Database of Systematic Reviews 2014;(9):CD002063.*

This meta-analysis reviewed 12 randomized or quasi-randomized trials comparing IVIG versus no treatment, placebo treatment, or plasma exchange in children and adults with Guillain-Barré syndrome. Treatment with either IVIG or plasma exchange was equally efficacious.

## 4. Migraine

**A Controlled Trial of Erenumab for Episodic Migraine**  
*N Engl J Med 2017;377:2123–2132.*

This multicenter randomized placebo-controlled trial randomized patients with episodic migraine to erenumab (a monoclonal antibody against the calcitonin gene-related peptide receptor) versus placebo. Patients who received erenumab administered subcutaneously monthly had significantly reduced migraine frequency and improved quality of life compared to placebo.

## 5. Deep Brain Stimulation

Deep brain stimulation targets are selected based on historical ablation targets as well as modern neuroimaging and neurophysiological mapping. Common targets include the subthalamic nucleus (STN) and globus pallidus internus (GPi) for Parkinson's disease, the ventral intermediate nucleus (VIM) of the thalamus for tremor, and the centromedian nucleus (CMPF) of the thalamus for epilepsy.

| **CMPF** | Centromedian nucleus |
