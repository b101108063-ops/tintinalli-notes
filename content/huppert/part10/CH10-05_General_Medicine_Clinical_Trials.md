## 1. Chronic Pain Management

* **Reference**: Pain Management With Opioids in 2019–2020. *JAMA* 2019;322(19):1912–1913.
* This paper offers guidance on the management of patients with chronic pain who are currently on opioid therapy.

---

## 2. Diet and Exercise

* **Reference**: Association of Step Volume and Intensity With All-Cause Mortality in Older Women. *JAMA Intern Med* 2019;179(8):1105–1112.
* **Study Design**: Cohort study of 16,741 women with a mean age of 72 years, measured steps per day over 7 days.
* **Findings**: Women who averaged 4,400 steps per day had significantly lower mortality rates during follow-up. Mortality rates progressively decreased with increasing steps, with a plateau at 7,500 steps per day.

## 3. EPIC Trial

* **Reference**: *JAMA Intern Med* 2019;179(11):1479–1490.
* **Study Design**: Population-based cohort study of 451,743 individuals from 10 countries in Europe.
* **Findings**: Higher all-cause mortality was found among participants who consumed 2 or more glasses per day of sugar-sweetened beverages compared to those who consumed less than 1 glass per month (HR 1.17, **95%** CI, 1.11–1.22, p < 0.001). Regular and diet soda is associated with mortality, especially cardiovascular disease.

## 4. Advanced Care Planning

* **Reference**: Advanced Directives and Outcomes of Surrogate Decision Making Before the Health and Retirement Study. *N Engl J Med* 2010;362(13):1211–1218.
* **Study Design**: This study used data from survey proxies in the Health and Retirement Study involving adults age 60 and older.
* **Findings**: Many patients required decision making near the end of life when most lacked the capacity to make decisions. Patients who had prepared advanced directives received care consistent with their preferences.

---

## 5. Preventative Health (ASPREE Trial)

* **Reference**: *N Engl J Med* 2018;379(16):1–10.
* **Study Design**: Multicenter, double-blind, randomized placebo-controlled trial that randomized healthy older adults to aspirin or placebo.
* **Findings**: Over almost 5 years of follow-up, rates of disability-free survival were similar between groups (21.5 vs. 21.2 events per 1000 person-years in aspirin and placebo groups, respectively). Aspirin was associated with higher incidence of major hemorrhage (8.6 vs. 6.2 events per 1000 person-years, respectively).

---

## 6. Colon Cancer Screening (COLONPREV)

* **Reference**: *N Engl J Med* 2018;379(16):1–10.
* **Study Design**: Multicenter, randomized, controlled non-inferiority trial that randomized over 50,000 patients to one-time colonoscopy or annual fecal immunochemical testing (FIT).
* **Findings**: In the interim analysis, FIT was non-inferior to baseline colonoscopy in detecting colorectal cancer. Ten-year outcomes will be reported in 2021.
