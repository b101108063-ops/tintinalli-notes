## 1. Diverticulitis: AVOD Trial

**Citation**: *British Journal of Surgery* 2012;99:532–539.

**Study design**: Non-blinded, placebo-controlled, randomized controlled trial comparing antibiotic therapy versus placebo for treatment of acute uncomplicated diverticulitis.

**Result**: Antibiotic treatment did not accelerate recovery or prevent complications or recurrence in acute uncomplicated diverticulitis.

---

## 2. Cholecystectomy: ACDC Trial

**Citation**: *Annals of Surgery* 2013;258:385–393.

**Study design**: Parallel-group, open-label, randomized controlled trial comparing early laparoscopic cholecystectomy versus delayed cholecystectomy for acute cholecystitis.

**Result**: Early surgery had lower morbidity and healthcare costs compared to delayed surgery.

---

## 3. Inflammatory Bowel Disease: SONIC Trial

**Citation**: *New England Journal of Medicine* 2010;362:1383–1395.

**Study design**: Double-blind randomized controlled trial assessing the safety and efficacy of infliximab monotherapy versus combination therapy with infliximab and azathioprine versus azathioprine monotherapy in patients with Crohn's disease who were naive to immunosuppressants and corticosteroids.

**Result**: Combination therapy with infliximab and azathioprine or infliximab monotherapy were more likely to achieve steroid-free remission compared to azathioprine monotherapy.

---

## 4. Functional Bowel Disorders: CBT Trial

**Citation**: *Gastroenterology* 2003;125:19–31.

**Study design**: Randomized controlled trial comparing cognitive behavioral therapy (CBT) versus education and desipramine (TCA) versus combined therapy for functional bowel disorders.

**Result**: Cognitive behavioral therapy is effective, and desipramine is effective when taken adequately and in certain clinical contexts.

---

## 5. Alcoholic Hepatitis: STOPAH Trial

**Citation**: *New England Journal of Medicine* 2015;372(17):1619–1628.

**Study design**: Prospective multicenter randomized, double-blind trial in 65 hospitals in the United Kingdom comparing prednisolone versus pentoxifylline versus both versus neither for severe alcoholic hepatitis.

**Result**: Neither prednisolone nor pentoxifylline reduced all-cause mortality at 28 days. Prednisolone was associated with a non-significant mortality benefit at 28 days but no long-term benefit.

---

## 6. Cirrhosis Management: Norfloxacin vs. Ceftriaxone

**Citation**: *Gastroenterology* 2006;131:1049–1056.

**Study design**: Randomized controlled trial assessing oral norfloxacin versus intravenous ceftriaxone for the prevention of bacterial infection in cirrhotic patients with upper gastrointestinal bleeding.

**Result**: Intravenous ceftriaxone was more effective than oral norfloxacin for infection prophylaxis in this population.
