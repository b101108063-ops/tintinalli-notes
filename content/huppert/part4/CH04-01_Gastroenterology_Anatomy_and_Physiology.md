# Anatomy and Physiology

## Structure and Innervation of the GI Tract

### Components

The GI tract includes:
- **Epithelial cells:** Specialized for absorption/secretion
- **Muscularis mucosa:** Changes surface area for absorption/secretion
- **Circular muscle:** Decreases the diameter of the lumen
- **Longitudinal muscle:** Shortens a segment of the GI tract

### Innervation

- **Extrinsic innervation:** May be parasympathetic (excitatory, via vagus/pelvic nerve) or sympathetic (usually inhibitory)
- **Intrinsic innervation:** Coordinates extrinsic signals and uses local GI reflexes to modify motility (via the myenteric plexus) and secretion/blood flow (via the submucosal plexus)

## The Neuroendocrine System of the Gut

### Classification of GI Regulatory Substances

| Type | Description |
|------|-------------|
| **Hormones** | Released from endocrine cells in the GI tract into the portal circulation; enter systemic circulation and act on target cells |
| **Paracrines** | Released from endocrine cells, diffuse locally, and act on target cells |
| **Neurocrines** | Synthesized by neurons in the GI tract, released by action potentials across the synaptic cleft, and act on target cells |

### GI Regulatory Substances

| Substance | Type | Location/Source | Stimulus | Action |
|-----------|------|----------------|----------|--------|
| **Gastrin** | Hormone | G cells in the stomach | High pH, stomach distention | Increases H⁺ secretion, gastric motility |
| **Gastrin-releasing peptide (GRP)** | Neurocrine | Released from vagus nerve; acts on G cells | — | Stimulates gastrin release |
| **Histamine** | Paracrine | Secreted by mast cells in the stomach | — | Increases H⁺ directly and by potentiating effects of gastrin/vagal nerve stimulation |
| **Cholecystokinin (CCK)** | Hormone | I cells in the small intestine | Fatty acids, amino acids | Stimulates pancreatic secretion, gallbladder contraction |
| **Secretin** | Hormone | S cells in the small intestine | Fatty acids, low pH | Increases pancreatic HCO₃⁻ and bile secretion |
| **Glucose-dependent insulinotropic peptide (GIP)** | Hormone | K cells in the small intestine | Fatty acids, amino acids, oral glucose | Decreases gastric acid secretion, increases insulin release |
| **Vasoactive intestinal polypeptide (VIP)** | Neurocrine | Released by parasympathetic ganglia in the intestine | Stomach distention, vagal nerve | Stimulates pancreatic HCO₃⁻ secretion, inhibits H⁺ secretion, promotes sphincter relaxation |
| **Somatostatin** | Paracrine | D cells | Vagal stimulation, low pH | Decreases gastric acid secretion, gallbladder contraction, and pancreatic secretion |

### Gastric Secretions

| Secretion | Produced By | Stimulus | Action |
|-----------|------------|----------|--------|
| **HCl** | Parietal cells in the stomach | Histamine, vagal stimulation (via ACh), gastrin | Decreases stomach pH to promote food digestion |
| **Pepsin** | Chief cells in the stomach | Vagal stimulation (via ACh), local acid | Stimulates protein digestion |
| **Bicarbonate (HCO₃⁻)** | — | — | Neutralizes acid |

## Vasculature of the GI Tract

### Arterial Supply to the Upper GI Tract

The celiac trunk gives rise to:
- **Common hepatic artery:** Supplies the liver, gallbladder, pancreas, and proximal duodenum
- **Left gastric artery:** Supplies the low esophagus and upper portion of the stomach
- **Splenic artery:** Supplies the spleen and short gastric arteries

### Vascular Supply to the Upper and Lower GI Tract

| Artery | Structures Supplied |
|--------|-------------------|
| **Celiac** | Low esophagus to proximal duodenum; liver, gallbladder, pancreas, spleen |
| **Superior mesenteric artery (SMA)** | Distal duodenum to proximal two-thirds of the transverse colon |
| **Inferior mesenteric artery (IMA)** | Distal one-third of the transverse colon to the upper portion of the rectum |
